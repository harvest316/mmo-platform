# Google Ads Campaign Structure -- auditandfix.com

Budget: A$200/month = ~A$6.50/day
Target market: Australia only
Landing page: https://auditandfix.com/scan
Bidding: Manual CPC (switch to Maximise Conversions after 30+ conversions/month)

---

## Campaign 1: Brand -- Audit and Fix

**Type:** Search
**Daily budget:** A$1.00
**Bidding:** Manual CPC, max A$0.50
**Geographic targeting:** Australia
**Language:** English
**Ad schedule:** All days, all hours (brand is cheap, always capture)
**Device targeting:** All devices, no bid adjustments
**Networks:** Search only (no Display, no Search Partners)

### Ad Group 1.1: Brand -- Exact

Keywords: brand terms (see keywords.csv)

Purpose: Protect brand from competitors bidding on our name. Expect very low CPCs (A$0.10-0.30). This campaign should spend almost nothing but captures anyone searching for us directly.

The brand campaign will likely underspend. Google will not charge more than A$1.00/day for brand, so the effective budget for non-brand is higher.

---

## Bidding Strategy Transition Plan

1. **Weeks 1-4:** Manual CPC. Learn which keywords convert, what CPCs look like.
2. **Weeks 5-8:** If 15+ conversions accumulated, switch Non-Brand campaigns to Maximise Conversions (no target CPA yet).
3. **Weeks 9-12:** If 30+ conversions, set target CPA. Target: A$15-25 per scan completion (email capture), A$50-80 per Quick Fixes sale.
4. **Ongoing:** Review every 2 weeks. If tCPA is limiting volume, raise it 10-15%. If overspending, lower it.

---

## Geographic Targeting Notes

- Target: Australia (all states and territories)
- Location option MUST be "Presence: People in or regularly in" -- NOT "Presence or interest"
- The "interest" setting will show ads to people outside Australia searching for Australian businesses, wasting budget
- No state-level bid adjustments initially (insufficient data at A$200/month)

Add these audiences in OBSERVATION mode (not targeting) to collect data:

- In-market: Web Design & Development
- Affinity: Business Professionals

After 4-6 weeks, review audience performance. If a segment converts 2x+ above average, consider adding a +20% bid adjustment.
