# Review Acquisition Campaign — Audit&Fix

**Goal:** Reach 5+ reviews on Google Business Profile and 5+ reviews on Trustpilot.
**Mechanism:** Free audit reports as genuine lead-gen, followed by a separate voluntary review ask.
**Decision reference:** DR-119

---

## 1. Compliance First — The Framing That Matters

Both Google and Trustpilot explicitly prohibit incentivised reviews. Their policies do not carve out
an exception for "honest" reviews — any offer of value connected to the act of leaving a review is
prohibited, regardless of sentiment neutrality.

ACCC (Australian Consumer Law) is slightly less strict: it allows incentivised honest reviews
provided the incentive is unconditional, disclosed, and sentiment-neutral. But Google/Trustpilot
operate their own terms on top of ACL — their ToS governs what stays on their platforms.

**The only safe structure:**

1. Deliver the free report with zero strings attached and zero mention of reviews.
2. Wait until the report has been delivered and presumably read (minimum 24-48 hours).
3. Send a separate follow-up that asks, voluntarily, if they'd be willing to share feedback.
4. The report must already be in their inbox before the review ask goes out — never conditional,
   never "you'll get the report once you leave a review."

This is not just a legal fig leaf. It is also better conversion mechanics: asking for a favour after
you have already given value (reciprocity) outperforms asking upfront.

## 2. Target Segment for Maximum Review Likelihood

Not all businesses will leave a review. Prioritise:

**Primary signals (highest propensity):**
- Businesses that already have 10+ Google reviews themselves. They understand the ecosystem,
  they have used it, and they are not intimidated by the process.
- Owner-operated businesses (not chains or franchises). The person who receives the email is the
  person who controls the Google Business Profile.
- Service businesses with a local reputation focus: tradies, dentists, physios, accountants,
  solicitors, cafes, gyms. They depend on reviews for new clients — they understand the value.
- Businesses with a score below 70 on our audit. They have the most to gain from the report
  (genuine value delivered) and are most likely to appreciate it.

**Secondary signals:**
- Businesses with a registered Google Business Profile that shows recent activity (responded to
  at least one review in the last 6 months). This signals the owner actively manages their profile.
- Businesses with an active Facebook page or Instagram. Social media active = digitally engaged =
  more likely to act on a digital ask.

**Avoid:**
- Businesses with 0 Google reviews (they may not have a GBP or may not understand the platform).
- Multi-location chains (reviews go to a head office, not the person you emailed).
- Businesses that look like they outsource their digital presence (agencies as intermediaries = low
  conversion and no authentic voice).

## 3. Review Mechanics — The Optimal Flow

### Step 1 — Initial outreach (email or SMS)
Offer a free audit. No mention of reviews. No conditions. This is a lead-gen offer.

### Step 2 — Report delivery
Send the completed audit report. Include a brief note: "Let me know if you have any questions about
anything in there." No ask yet.

### Step 3 — Review request (48 hours after delivery, separate message)
A short, personal-feeling follow-up. The report is already received. No pressure. The ask is framed
as helping future small businesses — not as a transaction.

### Step 4 — One reminder (optional, 5-7 days later)
One soft reminder only. If no response after this, do not push further. Mark the contact as
review-requested and move on.

## 5. Volume Planning

### Conversion funnel model (conservative):

| Stage | Rate | Count needed for 5 reviews |
|-------|------|---------------------------|
| Initial outreach sent | 100% | ~500 |
| Positive reply ("send me the report") | 10-15% | ~50-75 |
| Report delivered and opened | 80% of replies | ~40-60 |
| Review request opened | 60% open rate | ~25-36 |
| Actual review left | 15-25% of those who opened | 4-9 |

**Working target:** Send free audit offers to ~500 well-targeted businesses to generate 5+ reviews.
Plan for 150-200 reports actually delivered (accepts + self-serves from website scanner).

### Key lever: targeting quality

A poorly targeted list (low-review-count businesses, chains, unresponsive contacts) can push
conversion to review down to 5%. A well-targeted list (owner-operated, already active on Google,
local service businesses) can push it to 25-30%.

### Time estimate (Sydney/AEDT):

- Week 1: Outreach to 500 businesses
- Week 1-2: Reports delivered for positive replies (~50-75 reports)
- Week 2-3: Review requests go out 48h after each delivery
- Week 3-4: First 5 reviews should land, possibly sooner

If the pipeline is already generating these outreach contacts, this is essentially zero marginal
work — just add a "free report" offer path and tag the cohort for the review follow-up.

## 6. Trustpilot Setup

### Claiming the profile

1. Go to business.trustpilot.com
2. Search for "auditandfix.com" — a profile may already exist if anyone has reviewed it
3. Click "Claim your free profile" or "Claim your business page"
4. Create a Trustpilot business account using an @auditandfix.com email address (this is required
   for domain-match verification — otherwise manual verification is needed)
5. Once claimed, the profile shows "Claimed" status

### Verification

If the signup email matches the business domain (e.g. hello@auditandfix.com), verification is
automatic. If using a gmail or other external address, Trustpilot requires additional domain
verification (DNS TXT record or file upload).

Use an @auditandfix.com address for the business account signup — it avoids the verification step.

### Sending review invitations (free plan)

On the free Trustpilot plan, you can generate a unique review invitation URL:
`https://www.trustpilot.com/review/auditandfix.com`

This is a direct link to the review form for your domain. Drop this into the step 3 follow-up
alongside the Google review link. Offer both and let the recipient choose.

Do not use Trustpilot's "Invite Customers" automated email feature until you have a paid plan —
the free plan generates the link, you send it yourself.

### What Trustpilot prohibits (critical):

- Inviting only customers you believe will leave positive reviews (cherry-picking)
- Offering any incentive for leaving a review (discount, gift, payment, free service)
- Writing reviews yourself or having employees/friends review the business

You MUST invite all customers systematically (or at minimum, all customers who received a
delivered report), not selectively. Keep a record of who was invited so you can demonstrate this
if challenged.

## 7. Google Business Profile — No Physical Address

Audit&Fix is an online-only service. Here is the correct setup:

### Service Area Business (SAB) setup:

1. Go to business.google.com
2. Click "Add your business to Google"
3. Choose business category: "Web designer" or "Computer consultant" (closest match)
4. When asked "Do you want to add a location customers can visit?" — select NO
5. Select "I serve customers at their locations" or "I deliver goods and services to my customers"
6. Define your service area: select Australia-wide (or start with NSW/VIC/QLD)
7. Do not add a physical address — hide it. SABs can hide their address.

### What you will and will not get:

- You WILL get a Google Business Profile that shows up in search results for branded searches
  ("auditandfix.com review", "audit and fix website")
- You WILL be able to generate a direct review link
- You WILL NOT appear in the Google Maps "local pack" for generic searches without a strong
  local SEO footprint (this matters less — you want the reviews for credibility, not Maps ranking)
- You WILL NOT be penalised for hiding your address as an online service

### Getting your review link:

After setup: google.com/search?q=auditandfix.com — your GBP panel should show. Click "Ask for
reviews" to get your shortlink (format: g.page/r/[ID]/review). Use this in all review requests.

## 8. Additional Compliance Notes (Australian Context)

### What ACCC requires if you ever do run a conditional incentive (for reference):

If you ever change strategy and offer something in exchange (e.g., on a different platform that
allows it), ACCC requires:
- The incentive is available regardless of positive or negative review
- The reviewer is explicitly told this before writing their review
- The incentive is disclosed to readers of the review (e.g., "I received a free report in exchange
  for this review")

Google and Trustpilot both prohibit this regardless of ACCC compliance. So for Google and
Trustpilot, the DR-119 two-step structure (deliver first, ask separately) is the only viable path.

### Spam Act 2003 (AU):

All cold outreach emails must:
- Identify the sender clearly
- Include a functional unsubscribe mechanism
- Be sent from an Australian or overseas entity with a genuine business purpose
- Not be misleading about the nature of the communication (subject line must not be deceptive)

This is already handled in the 333Method outreach pipeline. The free-report campaign uses the same
infrastructure, so compliance is inherited.
